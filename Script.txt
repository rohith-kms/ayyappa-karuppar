TONAL THESIS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The two reference scenes work for opposite reasons; the craft is fusing them.

GANDALF at Helm's Deep earns its goosebumps through DEPRIVATION THEN RELEASE —
grinding hopelessness, a remembered promise, a single horse on a ridgeline
backlit by dawn, and only then the charge. The emotion is RESCUE.

THOR in Wakanda earns its roar through a HELD BREATH THEN DETONATION — the sky
goes dark and silent, everyone stops, a single beam of light, and the violence
arrives all at once with godlike disproportion. The emotion is AWE / DREAD.

We build Karuppar's entrance on Gandalf's HOPE ARCHITECTURE (despair -> promise
-> silhouette on the ridge) but detonate it with Thor's IMPACT PHYSICS (silence
-> light -> instant, overwhelming, disproportionate carnage). The cut between
those two halves is the whole reel.

MOVEMENT II — THE WRATH OF SHIVA  (0:32 – 0:50)
The promise. The audience earns the relief.
==============================================================================

[0:32 – 0:38]  KAILASH — ABSOLUTE STILLNESS
We open on total silence. The contrast makes the audience lean in.
COMPOSITION: Shiva, seated, snow-still, the cosmos turning slowly behind him.
Cool blue-white palette — the first clean, un-bloodied frame in the film. It
should feel like surfacing from underwater.

[0:38 – 0:46]  THE CLOSE-UP — THE BREATH BEFORE THE STRIKE
CAMERA: An extreme, patient push to a macro close-up of Shiva's face. We study
absolute serenity. The cosmos turning behind him slows, then stills entirely —
as if the universe itself has stopped breathing.
THE TURN: His eyes — closed in meditation for centuries — open. Not wide. A
slow, deliberate parting of the lids, and beneath them the pupils are no longer
calm but burning, banked coals catching their own draft. The jaw sets. A single
vein rises at the temple. The serenity has not broken — it has armed itself.
THE STRIKE: Pull back to a clean wide. Shiva, seated, lifts his right hand. The
palm — massive, ash-pale, scarred — rises with the patience of judgment and
comes down on his own thigh in a single sovereign blow. THE IMPACT lands as a
thunderclap that splits the sound design open: snow sheets off the peaks of
Kailash, stars in the cosmos behind him jolt out of their orbits, the serpent
at his neck flinches awake. From the place where palm met thigh, a column of
black fire erupts upward — silent for one frame, then roaring.
SOUND: A sub-bass tone building almost below hearing — then the thigh-strike
detonates through the mix like a struck bell the size of a mountain.
VO: "When Adharma will not stay fallen... Dharma does not answer gently."

[0:46 – 0:50]  THE FORGING
CAMERA: The frame whips up into the column of black flame rising from Shiva's
thigh — a churning vortex of cool black and white fire. A warrior form ASSEMBLES

cheroot glowing to life between clenched teeth.
