SECTION A — CULTURAL ANCHOR  (the highest-stakes section)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[ ]  Skin tones look South Indian / Dravidian. NOT European, NOT generic "warm
     tan", NOT olive Mediterranean, NOT pale Northern European.
[ ]  Garments are dhoti / veshti / angavastram. NOT tunics, NOT togas, NOT
     kilts, NOT chainmail hauberks, NOT plate armor over surcoats.
[ ]  Weapons are aruval, vel, kattar, curved Indian blades, leaf-shaped spear
     heads. NOT longswords, NOT broadswords, NOT claymores, NOT katanas, NOT
     Roman gladii, NOT Viking axes (unless asura — even then check the asura
     locked block).
[ ]  Armor (where present) is bronze-banded leather with brass studs, bone-and-
     leather, or temple-warrior style. NOT plate mail, NOT chainmail, NOT
     gambeson + tabard, NOT Spartan-style.
[ ]  Headgear is turbans, simple cloth wraps, or temple-iconography helms. NOT
     horned helmets, NOT knight visor helms, NOT Spartan crested helms, NOT
     samurai kabuto, NOT Norse mail coifs.
[ ]  Facial markings (vibhuti, tilak) are correctly placed where the locked
     block specifies them. Karuppar: three horizontal white ash stripes +
     single red vertical tilak. Ganas: white ash markings.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION C — COMPOSITION  (reference plate, not cinematic frame)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[ ]  Single isolated subject, clean neutral background (or simple grey). For
     environment plates: clean wide establishing shot with no characters.
[ ]  Full body / full subject visible in frame (no cropped legs, no cut heads
     unless intentional close-up reference).
[ ]  Even, diffuse lighting that shows the subject clearly. NOT dramatic
     chiaroscuro for plates (save that for sequence frames).
[ ]  Aspect ratio is 1:1 (square). Sequence frames switch back to 9:16.
[ ]  Front-facing OR 3/4 view (the most useful angle for later --cref).
[ ]  No motion blur, no flames or smoke obscuring the subject (unless that's
     part of the subject's iconography — e.g., Mahishi can be partially
     smoke-wreathed).
