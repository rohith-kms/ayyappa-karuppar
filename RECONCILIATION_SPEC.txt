==============================================================================
RECONCILIATION_SPEC.txt
Mechanical spec for syncing BIBLE LOCKED BLOCKS to the approved reference plates.
==============================================================================

>>> WHO RUNS THIS: a multimodal cheap model (e.g., Claude Haiku, GPT-4o-mini).
    The producer attaches all 14 reference plates listed below AND points the
    model at this spec + CHARACTER_BIBLE.txt + ENVIRONMENT_BIBLE.txt.

>>> TRIGGER PHRASE: "Read RECONCILIATION_SPEC.txt and RUN"
    Optional: "Read RECONCILIATION_SPEC.txt and RUN <subject>" — limit to one
    subject (e.g., "RUN Karuppar" or "RUN Battlefield").

>>> WHEN: after Stage 0 image generation is complete and the plates have been
    saved into their folders. Re-run any time a plate is regenerated.

>>> WHAT CHANGES: only the LOCKED BLOCK paragraphs inside CHARACTER_BIBLE.txt
    and ENVIRONMENT_BIBLE.txt. NEVER touches anything else.

THE PROCEDURE  (do this for EACH of the 14 subjects)
==============================================================================
STEP 1 — LOAD THE TEXT.
  Open the matching BIBLE file (CHARACTER_BIBLE.txt or ENVIRONMENT_BIBLE.txt).
  Find the subject's locked-block heading (per the Subject Map above) and
  extract its current LOCKED BLOCK — the paragraph that immediately follows
  the `LOCKED BLOCK:` line for that subject.

STEP 2 — LOOK AT THE PLATE.
  Carefully observe the corresponding image:
   - skin tone / hide texture / animal coat
   - eye color and luminosity
   - garment colors, patterns, and exact style (dhoti, veshti, angavastram,
     armor type)
   - weapon shape and materials
   - headgear / hair / horns / face markings
   - body proportions (height, build)
   - background palette (for environment plates)
   - signature features visible on the plate

STEP 3 — COMPARE.
  Identify every detail where the IMAGE differs from the TEXT. Be specific:
   - If the text says "amber-orange eyes" but the plate shows them as more
     "molten gold", that's a change.
   - If the text says "white sacred ash stripes" but the plate shows them more
     yellow/cream, that's a change.
   - If the text doesn't mention an ornament that's clearly on the plate
     (e.g., an arm band), ADD it.
   - If the text mentions something not actually visible on the plate (e.g.,
     "rudraksha beads" but no beads in frame), KEEP it — the plate may have
     omitted it but the locked block governs all future generations.

STEP 4 — WRITE THE REPLACEMENT LOCKED BLOCK.
  Produce one updated paragraph that follows the EXACT same structure and
  voice as the original. Apply the KEEP / CHANGE rules below.

STEP 5 — OUTPUT.
  See OUTPUT FORMAT below.

==============================================================================
KEEP / CHANGE RULES  (the spine of reconciliation)
==============================================================================

KEEP (do NOT modify, ever):
  - The CULTURAL ANCHOR language baked into the locked block. Phrases like
    "Tamil Hindu mythological", "Sanatan Dharma", "South Indian", "Dravidian
    martial culture", "Sabarimala tradition", "village-deity tradition" stay
    as-is.
  - The REVERENCE cues for Shiva and Ayyappa. Words like "serene", "calm
    transcendent", "divine", "fearless" stay.
  - Functional identity markers — Karuppar's cheroot, moustache, chains, vel,
    aruval; Mahishi's buffalo head; Shiva's crescent moon and Ganga; the
    Black Dog's amber eyes; the War-Horse's chain barding.
  - Paragraph length and voice — one paragraph, ~80-150 words, comma-chained
    descriptive style.

CHANGE (sync to what the plate actually shows):
  - Exact skin / hide / coat tone wording
  - Exact eye color and luminosity wording
  - Specific garment colors, patterns, layering
  - Specific armor materials, ornaments, jewelry
  - Headgear style, hair details, horn shape and length
  - Facial marking placement, color, count
  - Body proportions if the plate clearly disagrees with the spec
  - Background / sky / terrain palette (environment plates)
  - Any clearly visible detail the locked block did not mention

NEVER add to the locked block:
  - Camera framing, lighting direction, lens / depth-of-field language
  - Compositional notes (the locked block describes WHAT the subject IS, not
    how it's framed in a specific shot)
  - Cinematic descriptors ("dramatic chiaroscuro", "god-rays", etc. — those
    live in the STYLE token, not the locked block)
  - Negatives (those live in the NEGATIVES line below the locked block)
  - References to specific sequences

NEVER touch (outside the LOCKED BLOCK paragraph):
  - The `NEGATIVES (specific):` line and its body
  - The `REFERENCE:` line
  - The `NOTE:` line
  - `NEWBORN VARIANT`, `ASHING VARIANT`, weapon definitions, pose-reference
    sub-blocks
  - The cultural-anchor preamble at the top of the BIBLE file
  - Any other subject's locked block

==============================================================================
HARD RULES (do not break)
==============================================================================
  - Output text only — never write files directly. The producer will paste
    your replacement blocks into the BIBLE files.
  - If a plate is missing (no image attached for a subject), SKIP that subject
    and note "PLATE MISSING" in the summary table — do not invent.
  - If a plate is clearly wrong (e.g., the image shows a horse where Mahishi
    should be), flag it ("PLATE MISMATCH — image does not match subject") and
    do not produce a replacement block.
  - Never invent details not visible on the plate.
  - Never remove cultural-anchor or reverence language even if the plate is
    silent on it (the text governs future generations).
  - If "RUN <subject>" was used, process ONLY that subject and skip the rest.
